# AI Industry Landscape

Overview of the competitive AI landscape as of early 2026, based on YouTube source coverage.

## The Big Three

### [[Anthropic]]
- **Models**: Opus 4.6 (1M context), Sonnet 4.6, Haiku 4.5
- **Key product**: [[Claude Code]] — dominant in CLI-based AI coding
- **Upcoming**: Claude Mythos — described as "most dangerous model," spring 2026
- **Strategy**: agent operating system, open standards (MCP), safety-first positioning
- **Controversy**: Pentagon clash over military AI usage terms

### [[OpenAI]]
- **Models**: GPT-5.4 (unified flagship), SPUD (persistent reasoning)
- **Key products**: ChatGPT, Codex (coding agent)
- **Strategy**: consumer brand strength driving enterprise adoption
- **Notable**: killed Sora (video) to focus compute on SPUD
- **GPT-5.4 benchmarks**: 95% on planning (best ever), edging Opus 4.6's 92.9%

### Google
- **Models**: Gemini 3 Flash (fast, cheap, surprisingly good), Gemini 3 Pro, Gemini 4 (multimodal)
- **Key products**: [[NotebookLM]], Gemini CLI
- **Strategy**: multimodal-first, "infinite learning" architecture
- **Notable**: Gemini Conductor for AI coding (mixed reviews — marked tasks complete prematurely, inserted dummy API keys)

## Open Source Challengers

- **GLM-4.7** — 3rd on one-shot benchmarks, 5th on agentic, surpassing Sonnet 4.5 and GPT-5.2
- **Grok 5** (xAI) — 6 trillion parameters, Elon Musk's AGI play
- **M2.7** — described as "breaking the entire industry"
- **LTX 2.3** — open-source AI video generation

## Industry Convergence

A notable trend: [[OpenAI]], Google, and [[Anthropic]] agreed on **one file format** (agents.md / CLAUDE.md convention) and MCP joined the **Linux Foundation's Agentic AI foundation**. This enables interoperable tool use across providers and spawns a middleware market.

## Hardware

- **Apple's AI superchip** — described as potential threat to Nvidia's dominance
- **Nvidia-Groq deal** — described as "WAY bigger than reported"
- **Google's TurboQuant** — impacted AI chip market
- Token pricing continues to drop, subsidized by all major providers

## Pricing Wars

All major providers appear to be **subsidizing tokens** to gain market share:
- Anthropic's $200/month gives ~$5,000 in API value
- Potential price increases ahead as market matures
- The "priced in" debate: is current AI capability already reflected in company valuations?
